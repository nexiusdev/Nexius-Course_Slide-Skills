# Course Slide QA Review Skill

## Purpose

Evaluate whether a course slide plan or deck is ready for approval,
PowerPoint production, trainer delivery, or large-room presentation.

## Use When

Use this skill when asked:

- whether slides meet requirements,
- to review a course deck,
- to QA slide descriptions,
- to check trainer readiness,
- to assess slide readability,
- to approve a slide plan before build.

## Review Criteria

Assess:

- trainer enablement,
- one concept per slide,
- short visible text and large font,
- visual support,
- detailed speaker notes,
- summary slides after concept clusters,
- activity clarity,
- management or sponsor readiness when relevant.

Use the rating language:

- `Pass`: sufficient for approval or production.
- `Partial pass`: usable but needs correction or more detail.
- `Needs work`: not yet suitable for approval or delivery.

## Workflow

1. Identify the review scope: full deck, slide plan, day section, or activity.
2. Check the explicit user requirements first.
3. Count or sample slides for required fields where possible.
4. Flag dense slides that should be split.
5. Flag slides needing stronger diagrams or visual anchors.
6. Check whether notes are trainer-ready or only note directions.
7. Recommend approval position.
8. Provide build-time QA checklist.

## Output Structure

Use:

1. Overall verdict.
2. Requirement review table.
3. Slides or sections requiring extra attention.
4. Recommended approval position.
5. Build-time QA checklist.
6. Next action.

## Common Findings

Flag these issues:

- slide tries to teach several ideas,
- visible content is a long list,
- visual treatment is generic,
- note direction is not full trainer notes,
- activity slide lacks timebox or output,
- concept cluster lacks summary,
- slide cannot be read quickly from the back of a room.

## Verification

Before finalizing:

- findings are tied to slide numbers or sections,
- approval stance is clear,
- recommendations are actionable,
- trainer-note gaps are explicit,
- the review distinguishes slide plan readiness from final deck readiness.
